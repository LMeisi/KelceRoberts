FAQs

How Can We Help You?

An unexpected legal issue might arise from anywhere in life. It could be your business or personal life. When the unexpected happens, we at Kelce & Roberts fully understand how anxious and uncertain you are and we are here to help.

You may be wondering what to do next, if you should call the police, or if you should file a lawsuit or claim. Our FAQ section provides answers to some of the most common questions you might be having. Please take a look and it might help you plan out your next course of action. If you'd like to dive deeper, please reach out to us for a free consultation.

While the proceedings are similar, and case lengths have a limited range in some instances, every case is different.

Rest assured however, we will provide you with a detailed roadmap and timeline of all major events for your case during consultation, including filing claims, discovery, resolutions before trial, trial, verdict, and appeal, etc.

Once our client calls in for a consultation, we will be able to provide them with an exact list of what to bring.

Typical items to bring include your ID, any case-related legal documentation, any support evidence proofs, any case-related media files, police records (if applicable), etc.

It is all on a case-by-case basis. However, based on the type of your case, there may be options to settle out of court.

For example, while diverce can often be mediated, criminal law may have no other option besides attending a court hearing depending on the charges being brought.

It all depends on your case type and which state your lawsuit is filed in. For example, the statute of limitations on a product injury lawsuit in Illionis is two years, while in California, people have up to four years to file.

We will be able to tell you precisely how much time you have to file for your case during our first consultation.

We typically assign one attorney to one case at a time to ensure maximum attorney focus and effectiveness. However, in cases of large lawsuits, we could have multiple attorneys working on the same case together.

If you have concerns or specific requirements regarding attorney assignment, please reach out to us to discuss at any time. We are happy to accommodate your needs to the best of our abilities.
